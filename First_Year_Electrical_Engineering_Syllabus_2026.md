## 1EE100BS: ENGINEERING MATHEMATICS I
**Credits: 4 | L-T-P: 3-1-0 | Teaching Hours: 60**

### Course Objectives
- Develop mathematical foundations essential for electrical engineering analysis
- Apply calculus and linear algebra to solve engineering problems
- Understand complex numbers crucial for AC circuit analysis

### Course Outcomes
After completing this course, students will be able to:
- CO1: Apply concepts of matrices and linear algebra to solve system of equations
- CO2: Use differential calculus for optimization problems in engineering
- CO3: Apply integral calculus for computing areas, volumes, and physical quantities
- CO4: Utilize complex numbers for AC circuit analysis and phasor representation
- CO5: Solve ordinary differential equations modeling electrical circuits
- CO6: Apply Laplace transforms for circuit analysis

### UNIT I: Matrices and Linear Algebra (10 Hours)
- Types of matrices, Matrix operations
- Rank of a matrix, Echelon form
- System of linear equations: Consistency, Gauss elimination method
- Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors (with applications to stability analysis)
- Cayley-Hamilton theorem and its applications
- **Applications:** Network analysis, State-space representation basics

### UNIT II: Differential Calculus (10 Hours)
- Successive differentiation, Leibnitz's theorem
- Taylor's and Maclaurin's series expansions
- Partial differentiation, Euler's theorem on homogeneous functions
- Maxima and minima of functions of two variables
- Lagrange's method of multipliers
- **Applications:** Optimization in power systems, maximum power transfer

### UNIT III: Integral Calculus (10 Hours)
- Definite integrals, Beta and Gamma functions
- Multiple integrals: Double and Triple integrals
- Change of order of integration
- Applications: Area, Volume, Mean and RMS values
- **Applications:** RMS value of AC waveforms, Energy calculations

### UNIT IV: Complex Numbers and Functions (10 Hours)
- Complex numbers: Algebra, Polar and Exponential forms
- Euler's formula and its applications
- Complex functions, Analytic functions
- Cauchy-Riemann equations
- **Applications:** Phasor representation, AC circuit analysis, Impedance

### UNIT V: Ordinary Differential Equations (10 Hours)
- First-order ODEs: Separable, Exact, Linear equations
- Higher-order linear ODEs with constant coefficients
- Method of undetermined coefficients
- Variation of parameters
- **Applications:** RC, RL, RLC circuit transient analysis

### UNIT VI: Laplace Transforms (10 Hours)
- Definition, Properties, Laplace transforms of standard functions
- Inverse Laplace transforms
- Convolution theorem
- Solution of ODEs using Laplace transforms
- **Applications:** Circuit analysis, Transfer functions, Control systems basics

## 1EE101BS: ENGINEERING PHYSICS
**Credits: 3 | L-T-P: 3-0-0 | Teaching Hours: 45**

### Course Objectives
- Understand fundamental physics concepts relevant to electrical engineering
- Learn semiconductor physics for understanding electronic devices
- Study electromagnetic theory and wave propagation
- Explore quantum mechanics basics and modern physics applications

### Course Outcomes
After completing this course, students will be able to:
- CO1: Explain semiconductor properties and device behavior
- CO2: Apply electromagnetic principles to engineering problems
- CO3: Understand wave optics and its applications in communication
- CO4: Apply quantum mechanical concepts to explain modern device physics
- CO5: Describe superconductivity and its engineering applications
- CO6: Explain nanotechnology fundamentals and applications

### UNIT I: Semiconductor Physics (8 Hours)
- Crystal structure, Energy bands in solids
- Intrinsic and Extrinsic semiconductors
- Carrier concentration, Fermi level
- Carrier transport: Drift and Diffusion
- P-N junction: Formation, I-V characteristics
- Zener diode, LED, Photodiode fundamentals
- Hall effect and its applications
- **Applications:** Solar cells working principle

### UNIT II: Electromagnetic Theory (8 Hours)
- Electric and Magnetic fields review
- Maxwell's equations (integral and differential forms)
- Electromagnetic wave propagation
- Poynting vector and energy flow
- Reflection and refraction of EM waves
- Skin effect in conductors
- **Applications:** Transmission lines, Shielding, EMI/EMC basics

### UNIT III: Wave Optics and Fiber Optics (7 Hours)
- Interference: Thin film, Newton's rings
- Diffraction: Single slit, Grating
- Polarization of light
- LASER: Principle, Types (He-Ne, Semiconductor), Applications
- Optical fiber: Principle, Types, Numerical aperture
- Attenuation and dispersion in fibers
- **Applications:** Fiber optic communication, Laser applications in industry

### UNIT IV: Quantum Mechanics (7 Hours)
- Wave-particle duality, de Broglie hypothesis
- Heisenberg's uncertainty principle
- Schr√∂dinger wave equation (time-independent)
- Particle in a box, Quantum tunneling
- **Applications:** Tunnel diode, Scanning tunneling microscope

### UNIT V: Superconductivity and Applications (7 Hours)
- Superconductivity phenomenon, Critical parameters
- Type I and Type II superconductors
- BCS theory (qualitative)
- High-temperature superconductors
- Meissner effect
- **Applications:** SQUID, Superconducting magnets, Power cables, Maglev

### UNIT VI: Nanoscience and Nanotechnology (8 Hours)
- Introduction to nanoscale, Surface to volume ratio
- Quantum confinement effects
- Synthesis methods: Top-down, Bottom-up
- Carbon nanostructures: Fullerenes, Carbon nanotubes, Graphene
- Properties and applications of nanomaterials
- **Applications:** Nanoelectronics, Energy storage, Sensors

## 1EE103ES: FUNDAMENTALS OF ELECTRICAL ENGINEERING
**Credits: 3 | L-T-P: 3-0-0 | Teaching Hours: 45**

### Course Objectives
- Understand basic electrical quantities and their relationships
- Analyze DC and AC circuits using various techniques
- Understand magnetic circuits and electromagnetic induction
- Learn working principles of basic electrical machines and instruments

### Course Outcomes
After completing this course, students will be able to:
- CO1: Apply Ohm's law and Kirchhoff's laws to analyze DC circuits
- CO2: Analyze AC circuits using phasor techniques
- CO3: Apply network theorems to simplify circuit analysis
- CO4: Analyze magnetic circuits and understand electromagnetic induction
- CO5: Explain working principles of transformers
- CO6: Understand three-phase systems and power measurement

### UNIT I: DC Circuit Analysis (8 Hours)
- Electrical quantities: Charge, Current, Voltage, Power, Energy
- Resistance, Resistivity, Temperature coefficient of resistance
- Ohm's law, Series and Parallel circuits
- Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL) and Voltage Law (KVL)
- Mesh analysis and Nodal analysis
- Star-Delta transformation
- **Numerical problems mandatory**

### UNIT II: Network Theorems (7 Hours)
- Superposition theorem
- Thevenin's theorem
- Norton's theorem
- Maximum power transfer theorem
- Source transformation
- **Applications to practical circuits with numerical problems**

### UNIT III: AC Fundamentals (8 Hours)
- Generation of alternating EMF
- Waveform parameters: Amplitude, Frequency, Time period, Phase
- Average value, RMS value, Form factor, Peak factor
- Phasor representation of sinusoidal quantities
- Phasor algebra: Addition and subtraction
- j-operator, Complex impedance
- **Numerical problems on waveform analysis**

### UNIT IV: Single-Phase AC Circuits (8 Hours)
- Pure R, L, C circuits: Voltage-current relationships, Power
- Series R-L, R-C, R-L-C circuits
- Impedance, Admittance, Susceptance, Conductance
- Power in AC circuits: Active, Reactive, Apparent power
- Power factor and its significance
- Parallel AC circuits, Resonance (series and parallel)
- **Extensive numerical problem solving**

### UNIT V: Magnetic Circuits and Electromagnetic Induction (7 Hours)
- Magnetic quantities: Flux, Flux density, MMF, Reluctance, Permeability
- Analogy between electric and magnetic circuits
- Series and parallel magnetic circuits
- B-H curve, Hysteresis loop, Hysteresis and Eddy current losses
- Faraday's laws of electromagnetic induction
- Lenz's law, Self and mutual inductance
- Energy stored in magnetic field
- **Numerical problems on magnetic circuits**

### UNIT VI: Transformers and Three-Phase Systems (7 Hours)
**Transformers:**
- Construction, Working principle
- EMF equation, Transformation ratio
- Losses and efficiency
- Voltage regulation

**Three-Phase Systems:**
- Generation of three-phase EMF
- Star and Delta connections
- Line and Phase quantities
- Power in three-phase systems
- Power measurement (two-wattmeter method introduction)
- **Numerical problems**

### Text Books
1. D.P. Kothari & I.J. Nagrath, "Basic Electrical Engineering", McGraw Hill
2. V.N. Mittle & Arvind Mittal, "Basic Electrical Engineering", McGraw Hill

### Reference Books
1. B.L. Theraja, "A Textbook of Electrical Technology Vol-1", S. Chand

## 2EE101BS: ENGINEERING CHEMISTRY
**Credits: 3 | L-T-P: 3-0-0 | Teaching Hours: 45**

### Course Objectives
- Understand chemistry relevant to electrical engineering materials
- Learn about electrochemistry and energy storage systems
- Study corrosion and its prevention in electrical systems
- Explore materials for modern energy applications

### Course Outcomes
After completing this course, students will be able to:
- CO1: Explain electrochemical principles and battery technologies
- CO2: Analyze water quality and treatment methods
- CO3: Understand corrosion mechanisms and prevention techniques
- CO4: Describe polymer and insulating materials
- CO5: Explain fuel cells and hydrogen energy systems
- CO6: Understand nanomaterials for energy applications

### UNIT I: Electrochemistry and Batteries (8 Hours)
- Electrochemical cells, EMF, Nernst equation
- Types of electrodes, Reference electrodes
- Primary batteries: Zinc-carbon, Alkaline
- Secondary batteries: Lead-acid (detailed), Lithium-ion
- Battery parameters: Capacity, Energy density, Cycle life
- Battery Management Systems (BMS) basics
- **Applications:** UPS, Electric vehicles, Grid storage

### UNIT II: Water Chemistry and Treatment (7 Hours)
- Impurities in water, Hardness (types, units)
- Determination of hardness: EDTA method
- Water softening: Ion exchange, Zeolite process
- Boiler troubles: Scale, Sludge, Caustic embrittlement
- Desalination: RO, Electrodialysis
- Water for cooling in power plants

### UNIT III: Corrosion and Prevention (8 Hours)
- Types of corrosion: Dry, Wet, Galvanic, Pitting
- Electrochemical theory of corrosion
- Factors affecting corrosion rate
- Corrosion prevention: Material selection, Coatings
- Cathodic and Anodic protection
- Corrosion in electrical equipment
- **Applications:** Transmission towers, Substations, Underground cables

### UNIT IV: Polymers and Insulating Materials (7 Hours)
- Classification of polymers
- Addition and condensation polymerization
- Important polymers: PVC, PE, PP, PTFE (Teflon), Bakelite
- Conducting polymers (introduction)
- Electrical insulating materials: Classification, Properties

## 2EE103PC: BASIC ELECTRONICS ENGINEERING
**Credits: 3 | L-T-P: 3-0-0 | Teaching Hours: 45**

### Course Objectives
- Understand semiconductor device physics and characteristics
- Learn electronic circuit analysis fundamentals
- Understand operational amplifiers and applications
- Introduction to digital electronics basics

### Course Outcomes
After completing this course, students will be able to:
- CO1: Explain semiconductor device characteristics
- CO2: Analyze basic transistor circuits
- CO3: Design simple amplifier circuits
- CO4: Apply operational amplifiers for various applications
- CO5: Understand digital logic fundamentals
- CO6: Explain basics of power electronic devices

### UNIT I: Semiconductor Diodes and Applications (8 Hours)
- P-N junction diode: Characteristics, Specifications
- Diode circuits: Rectifiers (half-wave, full-wave, bridge)
- Filter circuits: Capacitor, LC, Pi filters
- Voltage regulation using Zener diode
- Special diodes: LED, Photodiode, Schottky, Varactor
- **Numerical problems on rectifiers and filters**

### UNIT II: Bipolar Junction Transistors (8 Hours)
- BJT: Construction, Operation (NPN, PNP)
- Configurations: CB, CE, CC
- Characteristics and parameters
- Biasing techniques: Fixed bias, Voltage divider bias
- Load line analysis, Operating point
- BJT as a switch
- **Numerical problems on biasing and load line**

### UNIT III: Field Effect Transistors (7 Hours)
- JFET: Construction, Operation, Characteristics
- MOSFET: Enhancement and Depletion types
- MOSFET characteristics and parameters
- Biasing of FETs
- Comparison: BJT vs FET vs MOSFET
- **Applications in modern electronics**

### UNIT IV: Amplifiers (8 Hours)
- Amplifier fundamentals: Gain, Bandwidth, Frequency response
- Single-stage CE amplifier: Analysis, Frequency response
- Multistage amplifiers: Cascading, Coupling methods
- Power amplifiers: Class A, B, AB, C (overview)
- Feedback amplifiers: Positive and Negative feedback
- Oscillators: RC phase shift, Wien bridge (principle)

### UNIT V: Operational Amplifiers (8 Hours)
- Ideal and practical op-amp characteristics
- Op-amp parameters: Slew rate, CMRR, Input offset
- Op-amp circuits: Inverting, Non-inverting amplifiers
- Summing amplifier, Difference amplifier
- Integrator, Differentiator
- Comparators, Schmitt trigger
- Active filters: Low-pass, High-pass (first order)
- **Numerical problems on op-amp circuits**

### UNIT VI: Digital Electronics and Power Devices (6 Hours)
**Digital Electronics:**
- Number systems: Binary, Octal, Hexadecimal
- Logic gates: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR
- Boolean algebra basics
- Simple combinational circuits

**Power Devices (Introduction):**
- SCR: Construction, Operation, Characteristics
- TRIAC, DIAC (overview)
- IGBT (introduction)
- **Applications in power control**

### Text Books
1. Robert L. Boylestad & Louis Nashelsky, "Electronic Devices and Circuit Theory", Pearson
2. David A. Bell, "Electronic Devices and Circuits", Oxford University Press

### Reference Books
1. Millman & Halkias, "Integrated Electronics", McGraw Hill
